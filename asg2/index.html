Structured Content vs. Semantic HTML

Structured content is planned out content that is ready to be compatible with any interface. Most of the time these come out to be in smaller packets of content data. For example: the top results from multiple other websites that come up from a Google search! On the other hand, semantic HTML is simply the mark ups and rankings of information within the HTML that help connect information. For example, a heading tag over a list tag communicates that the header pertains and relates to the information stored in that list.


How Do They Work Together?

Structured content and semantic HTML work together by offering different options for different functions within the same website. Semantic HTML is specific and gives you the information you were most likely after while the structured content gives you what the algorithm thinks you are after. Both are important functions for a website.


Why Is It Important?

Both are great ways of relaying content and information in different scenarios. Semantic HTML makes the information in a website concrete and the structured content makes that information more accessible.
